Stay Protected

Practical tips and advice to help you protect yourself from cyber threats in your everyday life.

Why Protection Matters

Most cyber attacks succeed because of simple, avoidable mistakes. Using weak passwords, clicking suspicious links, or ignoring software updates are among the most common reasons people get attacked.

The good news is that a few simple habits can dramatically reduce your risk. The tips below cover the most important steps you can take right now to protect yourself online.

Essential Protection Tips

Use Strong Passwords

A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

Avoid using obvious words like your name, birthday, or the word "password". Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of security to your accounts. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without the second factor.

Enable 2FA on all important accounts such as email, banking, and social media wherever possible.

Keep Software Updated

Software updates often include security patches that fix known vulnerabilities. Delaying updates leaves your device open to attacks that exploit those weaknesses.

Enable automatic updates on your operating system, browser, and apps so you never miss an important security fix.

Be Careful with Emails

Phishing emails are designed to look legitimate. Always check the sender's full email address, not just their display name, before clicking any links or downloading attachments.

If an email asks you to log in urgently or provide personal information, treat it with suspicion and visit the website directly instead.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi networks are often unsecured, making it easy for attackers to intercept your data. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts such as banking or email on public networks.

If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection and protect your data.

Back Up Your Data

Regular backups protect you from ransomware attacks and accidental data loss. If your files are encrypted by ransomware, a recent backup means you can restore everything without paying.

Follow the 3-2-1 rule: keep 3 copies of your data, on 2 different storage types, with 1 copy stored offsite or in the cloud.

Use a Password Manager

A password manager securely stores all your passwords in one place, encrypted behind a single master password. This means you only need to remember one strong password.

Popular options include Bitwarden, 1Password, and Dashlane. Most offer free plans that are more than sufficient for personal use.

Install Antivirus Software

A reputable antivirus program can detect and remove malware before it causes serious damage. It acts as a safety net catching threats that slip through other defences.

Keep your antivirus software up to date and run regular scans. Many operating systems include built-in antivirus tools that are free and effective.
